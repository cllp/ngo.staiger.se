About

The work chose me as much as I chose it

I'm a technology architect who spent two decades building systems for Fortune 500 companies — and discovered that the most meaningful work happened in crisis zones, refugee corridors, and underserved communities.

Somewhere along the way, in a field hospital in Central Africa or a refugee transit point in the Balkans, I realized something: the same architectural thinking that powers enterprise platforms can transform how humanitarian organizations operate. The difference isn't in the technology. It's in what you choose to build it for.

Our planet faces challenges that won't be solved by people optimizing for quarterly returns. Climate change, displacement, health inequity, access to information — these are the problems that demand our best thinking and deepest commitment.

The mission comes first. The compensation comes second. I want to work with organizations that are making the world better — and could use someone with deep technical experience, proven humanitarian field credentials, and a genuine commitment to impact.

I write this from Gotland, an island in the Baltic Sea. Remote work isn't a trend for me — it's how I've delivered results across continents, time zones, and cultures for years.
